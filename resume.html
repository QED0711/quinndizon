About Me:

I find the act of creating art and tools for others to experience or interact with, to be a fascinating and rewarding process. Composition came to me as the natural outgrowth of my love of music, and my desire to create. In recent years, I have become more involved with technology and computer programming, and have used my skills with programs like Max MSP to explore new ways of creating musical experiences.

ELECTRONIC INSTALLATIONS & PROJECTS:

Score Recognition Application

Allows users to quickly and easily generate random scores from a list of composers. Possible works can be filitered by genre and time period. This application was created to help graduate students at the University of Maryland School of Music study for the comprehenzive exams. The application can be accessed here.

(Google Chrome or Firefox browsers recommended)

TEMPO Maryland Day Installation

Inspired by minimalist indeterminate music, such as Terry Riely's In C, the installation generates small musical fragments to be performed by a live ensemble. Audience members interact with the installation via a touch screen interface, and influence the shape of the unfolding composition in real time. The installation is written in Max MSP and javacript. 

Files for this installation are available on Github

Medium: An interactive Sound Installation Presented by TEMPO

Interacting with an onsite acoustic piano, festival attendees recorded over 120 audio fragments. These fragments underwent spectral processing, and were continuously looped through a pair speakers pointed towards the sounding board of the piano. The resulting sound was a unique mix of acoustic and electronic music. The installation is written in Max MSP
